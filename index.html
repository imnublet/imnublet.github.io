About

the part the model can't infer

I like problems where the maths is unforgiving and the stakes are physical — a heartbeat, a stride, a spike train.

The throughline is signals. I trained in AI at TU Delft, then spent an exchange on computational neuroscience and neuroinformatics across ETH and UZH, learning to take the brain seriously as a system. Since then, mostly health-tech: wearables, radar, the messy business of measuring a body that won't hold still.

Off the clock I play first-team football and coach youth — which is the same job as engineering, really: notice the signal, ignore the noise, and make the next decision before you have all the data.
