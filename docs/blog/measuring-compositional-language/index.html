How Do You Measure Whether a Language Is Compositional?

When two neural agents invent a language to solve a game, is it compositional or a lookup table? Three metrics — topsim, posdis, bosdis — try to answer.

2026-07-18 8 min Growing Meaning Spaces emergent-communication · compositionality · information-theory

Put two neural networks in a referential game — one sees an object and sends a sequence of symbols, the other has to pick that object out of a lineup — and, given enough rounds, they will invent a language. The uncomfortable question my BSc thesis kept circling is: what kind of language? A compositional one, where symbols recombine to mean new things? Or a giant brittle lookup table that happens to work on the training set and shatters the moment the world grows? You cannot eyeball the answer. You have to measure it.

“Growing meaning spaces” is the experiment in the title: systematically enlarge the space of things the agents must talk about — more attributes, more values per attribute — and watch whether compositionality emerges and holds as the pressure to generalize increases.

§ 01 The gameEmergent communication in one paragraph

A sender observes a structured input — an object encoded as attribute/value pairs, say (colour = blue, shape = triangle, size = large) — and emits a discrete message of length L over a vocabulary of size V. A receiver reads the message and must identify the referent among distractors. Both are trained end-to-end; because the channel is discrete, gradients come through REINFORCE or a Gumbel-softmax relaxation. Nothing tells them how to encode meaning — a protocol simply emerges from the pressure to be understood. The meaning space is the full grid of attribute/value combinations, and “growing” it means adding attributes or values so that space expands combinatorially.

§ 02 Topographic similarityDo nearby meanings get nearby messages?

The oldest and most intuitive metric. A language is compositional if similar meanings map to similar messages — change one attribute, change a little of the message. Topographic similarity (topsim) makes that precise: take every pair of objects, measure their distance in meaning space (Hamming over attributes) and their distance in message space (edit distance over symbols), and compute the rank correlation between the two lists of distances.

High topsim means the geometry of meaning survives the trip through the channel. It is a good smell-test — but it is a single scalar over the whole language and can be fooled, which is why it needs company.

§ 03 DisentanglementWhich symbol carries which attribute?

Chaabouni et al. (2020) sharpened the question with two information-theoretic metrics that ask where each attribute’s information actually lives in the message.

Positional disentanglement (posdis) asks whether each position in the message is dedicated to a single attribute. For symbol position j, rank the attributes by mutual information with that symbol; the position is “clean” if the top attribute dominates the second:

Bag-of-symbols disentanglement (bosdis) asks the same question but throws away order: it uses symbol counts rather than positions, rewarding languages where each symbol in the vocabulary maps to a single attribute regardless of where it appears. Together the two catch different compositional strategies — positional grammar versus an order-invariant “bag” code — that topsim alone blurs into one number.

§ 04 The experimentWhat growing the space reveals

With three lenses on compositionality, the thesis question becomes measurable: as the meaning space grows, do topsim, posdis, and bosdis rise, and — crucially — do they predict generalization to held-out attribute combinations the agents never saw in training? So I tracked all three against generalization accuracy and against each other as the space expanded.

The recurring lesson of this literature — and of the plots that came out of these runs — is that the three metrics do not move in lockstep. A language can score well on one and poorly on another; a high topsim doesn’t guarantee clean positional structure. Compositionality is not a single axis, and the honest thing to report is a profile across metrics rather than one headline number. The final arbiter is always the same: does the protocol generalize to combinations it was never trained on?

§ 05 TakeawaysWhat I’d tell my earlier self

  • Compositionality is multi-dimensional. Report topsim and a disentanglement metric — one scalar hides the failure modes of the other.
  • Mutual-information metrics say where meaning lives in a message; correlational metrics say whether geometry is preserved. You want both.
  • Generalization to unseen combinations is the ground truth. A metric is only useful insofar as it predicts it.
  • Growing the meaning space is the right stress test: compositional structure is exactly what should pay off as the world gets bigger.
